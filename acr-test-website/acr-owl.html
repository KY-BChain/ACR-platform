🧠 Live SWRL Rules (Human-Readable)

High-Risk Detection Rule:
If a patient has imaging studies where both texture analysis shows values above 0.8 AND shape irregularity exceeds 0.75, then automatically classify this case as HIGH RISK and trigger urgent review protocols.
Patient(?p) ∧ hasImagingStudy(?p, ?study) ∧ hasRadiomicsFeature(?study, ?feature) ∧ hasTextureValue(?feature, ?texture) ∧ hasShapeValue(?feature, ?shape) ∧ swrlb:greaterThan(?texture, 0.8) ∧ swrlb:greaterThan(?shape, 0.75) → hasRiskLevel(?feature, HighRisk)
Age-Based Priority Rule:
When a patient over 40 years old receives a HIGH RISK classification from imaging analysis, immediately flag their case for urgent medical review and notify the clinical team within 24 hours.
Patient(?p) ∧ hasAge(?p, ?age) ∧ hasImagingStudy(?p, ?study) ∧ hasRadiomicsFeature(?study, ?feature) ∧ hasRiskLevel(?feature, HighRisk) ∧ swrlb:greaterThan(?age, 40) → requiresUrgentReview(?p, true)
Federated Learning Eligibility:
Include patient data in federated learning only if: data quality score exceeds 90%, patient has provided explicit consent for research participation, and the imaging study meets technical quality standards.
Patient(?p) ∧ hasImagingStudy(?p, ?study) ∧ hasDataQuality(?study, ?quality) ∧ hasPrivacyConsent(?p, true) ∧ swrlb:greaterThan(?quality, 0.9) → eligibleForFederatedLearning(?p, true)
